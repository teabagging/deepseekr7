questionA bucket that is (frac{2}{3}) full contains 9 litres of maple syrup. What is the capacity of the bucket, in litres?

answerFirst, I recognize that the bucket is two-thirds full and contains 9 liters of maple syrup.To find the total capacity of the bucket, I can set up an equation where two-thirds of the capacity equals 9 liters.Let ( C ) represent the capacity of the bucket. The equation becomes:[frac{2}{3}C = 9]To solve for ( C ), I multiply both sides of the equation by the reciprocal of (frac{2}{3}), which is (frac{3}{2}):[C = 9 times frac{3}{2} = 13.5]Therefore, the capacity of the bucket is 13.5 liters.
