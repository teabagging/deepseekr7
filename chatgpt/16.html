questionEmily, Olivia, and Ava are working at a restaurant, every day for 9 hours. Each of them is serving 5 tables per hour. One day Olivia had to attend a meeting, so she was working only for 7 hours, and Ava had to take a longer break, so she worked for 8 hours. How many tables did all three of these women serve that day in total?

answerFirst, I need to determine how many tables each person served individually and then sum those amounts to find the total number of tables served by all three.Emily worked the full 9 hours, serving 5 tables each hour. So, Emily served 9 multiplied by 5, which equals 45 tables.Olivia had to attend a meeting and only worked for 7 hours. At 5 tables per hour, Olivia served 7 multiplied by 5, totaling 35 tables.Ava took a longer break and worked for 8 hours. Therefore, Ava served 8 multiplied by 5, which is 40 tables.Adding up the tables served by each person: 45 (Emily) + 35 (Olivia) + 40 (Ava) equals 120 tables in total.
