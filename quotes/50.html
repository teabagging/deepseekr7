questionJordan is a hockey goalie. In the first period of a game, he blocked four shots. In the second period, he blocked twice as many shots. In the third period, he blocked three fewer than in the second period. When the game ended after the fourth period, he had blocked 21 shots in all. How many did Jordan block in the fourth period?

answerFirst, I note that Jordan blocked 4 shots in the first period.In the second period, he blocked twice as many shots as the first period, which is 4 multiplied by 2, resulting in 8 shots.For the third period, he blocked three fewer shots than in the second period. Therefore, he blocked 8 minus 3, which equals 5 shots.Adding up the shots blocked in the first three periods: 4 (first period) + 8 (second period) + 5 (third period) = 17 shots.Since the total number of shots blocked in the game is 21, the number of shots blocked in the fourth period is 21 minus 17, which equals 4 shots.
